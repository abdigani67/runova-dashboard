# Clinic-Owner Validation Interview Script
### AI Instagram DM automation for UK aesthetic clinics — pre-build validation

**Goal of this script:** resolve the two evidence gaps that the market research could *not* prove —
1. **Channel split** — do enquiries actually arrive via Instagram DM, or via phone / WhatsApp / web / Google? (Research Q3, refuted)
2. **Willingness to pay** — will a clinic pay **£800 setup + £300/month**, ~6× their PMS baseline? (Research Q6, refuted)

**Target:** 15–20 UK aesthetic clinic owners/managers (Botox, fillers, skin). Mix of solo practitioners and 2–5 chair clinics. 20–25 min each.

**Rules for a clean signal:**
- Ask about **past behaviour**, not hypothetical interest ("What did you do last week?" not "Would you like…?").
- **Never pitch first.** Pitching contaminates every answer after it. Discovery first, price last.
- Shut up after each question. Let silence pull out the real answer.
- Record numbers verbatim. Don't round in your head.

---

## Section A — Warm-up & context (2 min)
*Purpose: relax them, classify the clinic.*

**Q1.** Tell me about your clinic — how long running, how many practitioners, and what treatments make up most of your revenue?

**Q2.** Roughly how many *new-client* enquiries do you get in a typical week? (You want a number. If they hesitate: "More like 5, 25, or 100?")

---

## Section B — Channel split (the #1 gap) (6 min)
*Purpose: kill or confirm the Instagram-first premise. This is the most important section — do not rush it.*

**Q3.** Think about the last 10 new enquiries you got. Where did each one come in — Instagram DM, WhatsApp, phone call, website form, Google, walk-in, referral? *(Push for an actual count out of 10. Write down the split.)*

**Q4.** Which of those channels gives you the **best clients** — the ones who actually book and spend, not just tyre-kickers?

**Q5.** When an enquiry lands on Instagram specifically — who sees it, and how fast does someone typically reply? What about evenings and weekends?

> 🎯 **Decision gate:** If Instagram DM is *not* in the top 2 channels for most clinics, the Instagram-only product is mispositioned. Flag it — you may need a multi-channel (WhatsApp + IG + web) wedge instead.

---

## Section C — The pain (4 min)
*Purpose: confirm the pain is felt and costs money — in their words, not yours.*

**Q6.** What happens to an enquiry that comes in when you're in a treatment, closed, or it's the weekend? Walk me through a real recent example.

**Q7.** Have you ever realised you lost a booking because nobody replied fast enough — or replied at all? How often do you think that happens? What's a booking like that worth to you?

**Q8.** What do you currently do to chase enquiries that don't book the first time — any follow-up? Manual or automated?

---

## Section D — Current tools & spend (the #2 gap) (5 min)
*Purpose: establish the real software/marketing budget and the £300 reference points.*

**Q9.** What software do you pay for to run the clinic — booking, CRM, marketing? Roughly what does each cost you per month?

**Q10.** Outside software, what do you spend on getting new clients — ads, an agency, a social media person? Monthly ballpark?

**Q11.** When you've bought a new tool or service for the clinic, what made you say yes — and what's made you cancel something?

---

## Section E — Price reaction (last — never first) (4 min)
*Purpose: test £800 + £300/mo against real tolerance. Frame as recovered revenue, not a tool.*

**Setup line (say this verbatim):**
> "Some clinics tell us they lose a booking or two a month just because enquiries don't get answered fast enough. Imagine a service that automatically replies to every Instagram DM and enquiry within seconds, day or night, answers common questions, and pushes people to book — so nothing slips. It's £800 to set up and £300 a month."

**Q12.** *(Watch their face before they speak.)* What's your honest first reaction to that price?

**Q13.** At £300/month, how many extra bookings a month would it need to win for that to be a no-brainer for you? *(They'll usually say 1–2. Compare to their treatment value from Q7.)*

**Q14.** If you were going to say no to this, what would the reason be?

**Q15.** *(Commitment test — the realest signal in the whole call.)* If this existed today, would you want to be one of the first to try it? *(Optional hard test: "I'm onboarding a small founding group at £X — can I put you on the list?" A "yes + calendar slot" beats any verbal enthusiasm.)*

## How to read the results (decision rules)
- **GO (STRONG):** IG DM is top-2 channel for ≥60% of clinics **AND** ≥40% react neutral-or-better to £300/mo **AND** ≥5 say yes to a founding slot.
- **PIVOT:** Pain + spend confirmed, but enquiries are multi-channel → reposition as multi-channel enquiry automation (IG + WhatsApp + web), keep the price.
- **RECONSIDER PRICE:** Strong interest but consistent recoil at £300 → test £150–£200/mo or value-based/per-booking pricing.
- **NO-GO:** Pain not felt, or losing bookings isn't recognised as a problem → the product solves a problem they don't have.
